01 Why This Matters

Non-adherence is the largest preventable crisis in chronic disease

An estimated 125,000 deaths and $300 billion in avoidable healthcare costs occur annually in the United States alone — not from treatment failure, but from patients not taking their medications as prescribed.

Between 30% and 50% of patients with chronic conditions do not adhere to their prescribed regimens. Yet most interventions treat all non-adherence the same — a critical error with measurable consequences.

Scala Carta Foundation exists to change that by funding and disseminating the research infrastructure that makes precise, behavioral phenotyping of non-adherence possible at scale.

Intentional Non-Adherence (INA)
The patient chose not to

Driven by beliefs, distrust, perceived side effects, or calculated risk. Requires motivational and behavioral intervention, not reminders.

Unintentional Non-Adherence (UNA)
The patient wanted to

Caused by forgetfulness, complexity, access barriers, or system failures. Requires structural support and simplification.

MAP (Multidimensional Adherence Parameters) is the validated instrument that distinguishes INA from UNA, generating PEACS phenotypes that drive targeted intervention. Developed by Philip Morisky, MAP is the scientific core of TESSERA GRC.

TESSERA governs the world's only multi-site, multi-country MAP normative dataset. Members contribute aggregate data; TESSERA publishes validated phenotype benchmarks under open-access terms that any researcher can cite, replicate, or build on.

03 Research Equity

Science must travel where the burden is highest

The global burden of medication non-adherence falls disproportionately on low- and middle-income countries, where chronic disease rates are rising fastest and research infrastructure is thinnest. TESSERA GRC was designed with this asymmetry in mind.

Through our LMIC Grant Pathway and Research Contribution License (RCL), researchers at qualifying institutions can access full TESSERA membership, including MAP instrument access and co-authorship on normative studies, at no cost.

Donor support is what makes this possible. Every contribution to Scala Carta Foundation directly funds the participation of a researcher who would otherwise be excluded from the global evidence network.

Community · TESSERA Gateway Program

MAP assessment beyond the clinic walls.

The TESSERA Community Gateway is a lightweight, browser-based kiosk tool that allows community pharmacy networks, mobile outreach programs, and telehealth services to administer the MAP assessment without a full ATLAS platform subscription.

Each participating site is provisioned with a unique site code that serves as its credential. No patient login. No installation. Any browser, any device. A pharmacy staff member starts the session and the patient completes the 8-question MAP assessment at the counter or kiosk.

Assessments submitted through the Gateway contribute to the ATLAS global MAP dataset, plotted on the live adherence map by site coordinates. Data is governed under the TESSERA GRC compliance framework for the applicable jurisdiction.

✓ Available to Institutional Partner members and above
✓ Deployed per site via a provisioned SSM profile — no IT integration required
✓ Supports 20+ languages with automatic locale detection
✓ Patient receives downloadable phenotype report on completion
✓ Network-level dashboard for pharmacy chains and multi-site operators
✓ Assessments appear as dots on the live ATLAS global MAP globe
Gateway Use Cases
Community Pharmacy Networks
Assess patients at point of dispensing. No clinical setting required. Capture unbiased, real-world adherence phenotypes at scale.
Mobile Outreach Programs
Deploy at health fairs, community clinics, and pop-up care sites. Works on any tablet or laptop with a browser — no app, no installation.
Telehealth Services
Share the gateway link with patients before a virtual appointment. MAP phenotype data is available in the clinician queue before the call begins.

TESSERA  ·  Research Contribution License

Your institution restricts
external platforms or subscriptions.

Some researchers at funded universities and teaching hospitals cannot use external platforms due to ethics or data management agreements, or cannot pay for software subscriptions under their grant terms. This pathway exists for exactly that situation.

The Research Contribution License is a data exchange, not a discount. You receive the full instrument package and a formal letter of permission. In return, you submit a standardized aggregate data summary after data collection is complete. That summary contains no patient identifiers and is designed to fall outside the scope of your ethics documents and data management agreement by default.

You receive
✓ Validated MMAS-8 instrument in your language version
✓ Scoring key and administration documentation
✓ Formal letter of permission naming your study (citable in ethics submissions and publications)
✓ TESSERA Research Associate acknowledgment (citable in thesis and publications)
✓ Your institution named as a contributor in the TESSERA Normative Dataset
You submit (within 90 days of collection)
→ Completed TESSERA Normative Contribution Template (Excel)
→ Item-level Q1-Q8 responses, condition, country, age range, education level
→ No patient names, ID numbers, dates of birth, or any other identifier
→ Upload directly on this page — no ATLAS account required
GDPR / UK DPA note: The contribution template contains no personal data as defined under GDPR Art. 4(1) or UK DPA 2018. No Patient ID field exists in the template. Age ranges, not exact ages, are used. Submission does not require ethics amendment or DPA review. If your data governance officer requires written confirmation, contact philip.morisky@scalacartafoundation.org.
